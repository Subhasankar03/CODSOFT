How the Math Works

Cosine Similarity Formula

Measures the cosine of the angle between two multi-dimensional vectors. It evaluates directional orientation rather than magnitude.

Cosine Similarity = (A • B) / (||A|| * ||B||)
= Σ(A_i * B_i) / [ √Σ(A_i^2) * √Σ(B_i^2) ]

A similarity score of 1.0 means identical direction (complete preference match); 0.0 means orthogonal vectors (no preference similarity).

Content-Based Profiling

We create a User Preference Vector based on your high ratings (4-5 stars) and active genre pills. Then, we calculate the Cosine Similarity between this profile vector and each movie's binary genre vector.

Movie vectors contain 1 if the movie belongs to the genre, and 0 otherwise.

User-Based Collaborative Filtering

We calculate the rating overlap similarity between your profile and each of our 5 mock users. We then predict your rating for a movie you haven't seen by taking the weighted average of other users' ratings, weighted by their similarity to you:

Predicted Rating = Σ(Similarity_u * Rating_u) / Σ(|Similarity_u|)
